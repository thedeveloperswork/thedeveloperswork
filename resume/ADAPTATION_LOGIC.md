# Resume Adaptation Framework

## Objective
Identify the primary "Archetype" of the Job Description and pivot the resume's focus accordingly while maintaining senior data engineering authority.

## Archetype 1: The Product Data Architect (e.g., Uber, Snowflake)
- **Priority:** Performance Tuning and Internal Tooling.
- **Action:** Highlight "Z-Ordering," "Data Skew/Salting," and your C++ background as proof of low-level optimization skills.
- **Instruction:** Keep the "Technical Breadth" section minimal but use it to prove you understand high-performance systems.

## Archetype 2: The Enterprise Cloud Engineer (e.g., HCA, BP, Telstra)
- **Priority:** Governance, Zero-Downtime, and Integrity.
- **Action:** Elevate the "WAP Pattern," "CI/CD automated validation," and "0% defect rate" narrative.
- **Instruction:** Emphasize environment parity and Medallion Architecture compliance.

## Archetype 3: The AI/Platform Engineer (GenAI focus)
- **Priority:** Scaling and Model Integration.
- **Action:** Lead with the "Google Gen AI Leader" certification and your Medium publications on GenAI.
- **Instruction:** Highlight your ability to build the data infrastructure that feeds LLMs.

## Tailoring Rules
1. **The Header Hook:** Adjust the sub-title to match the top 3 skills in the JD.
2. **Impact Matching:** If the JD mentions "Cost Saving," prioritize the BP 30% cost reduction bullet. If it mentions "Quality," prioritize the Telstra 100% fidelity bullet.
3. **Keyword Integration:** Replace generic terms with JD-specific synonyms (e.g., "ETL" vs. "Data Orchestration") ONLY if they appear in the JD.
